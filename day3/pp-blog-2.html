Puppy Problems Webinar: Be on Guard for Resource Guarding

When a dog displays resource guarding tendencies as a puppy it can lead to more harmful or even dangerous behavior as an adult. Resource guarding can include being possessive of:

  • Food or treats
  • A particular toy (or toys)
  • A spot such as a bed or feeding area
  • An individual

Basically, resource guarding is when your dog acts possessive any thing, place, or person.

Signs of Resource Guarding

Signs that a dog is being possessive of something can include:

  • Growling
  • Snapping
  • Going stiff
  • Whining

Basically any sign of distress or aggression when you approach the item, place, or person your dog is guarding

Resource guarding is very common

It is common because in the wild, if your dog didn't resource guard he or she wouldn't survive. However, it is not "polite" or "healthy" behavior for dogs that live with humans.

The best time to address your puppy's resource guarding is when they are young!
